Bhasha-Rupantarika: Algorithm-Hardware Co-design Approach
For Multi-lingual Neural Machine Translation

Tanushree Dewangan∗, Mukul Lokhande∗, Tejas Chaudhari∗, Mohd Sharik Mansoori∗,
Adam Teman§, Senior Member, IEEE, Damayanti Lokhande, Santosh Kumar Vishvakarma∗, Senior Member, IEEE.

∗NSDCS Research Group, Dept. of Electrical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Indore, India.
§EnICS Labs, Faculty of Engineering, Bar Ilan University, Ramat Gan 5290002, Israel.

This work proposes Bhasha-Rupantarika, an algorithm-hardware co-design approach for multi-lingual neural machine translation. The framework leverages a dual-direction NLLB (No Language Left Behind) model to enable translation between Indian and overseas languages using a single unified model.

The design supports adaptive precision processing with INT4/FP4, FP8, and BF16 formats, enabled through reconfigurable hardware for resource-efficient implementation. This adaptability allows the system to dynamically balance accuracy and efficiency, making it well-suited for natural language processing workloads under diverse hardware constraints.

To ensure transparency and reproducibility, the complete codebase and experimental results are released publicly, empowering designers and researchers to readily adopt, extend, and build upon this work.
